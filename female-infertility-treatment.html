Female Infertility Treatment

What is Female Infertility Treatment?

Female infertility treatment encompasses a range of diagnostic and therapeutic interventions aimed at addressing the causes of infertility in women. Infertility is defined as the inability to conceive after 12 months of regular, unprotected intercourse (or six months for women over 35). Treatments are personalized based on the underlying causes, such as ovulation disorders, tubal blockages, endometriosis, or uterine abnormalities, with the goal of helping women achieve successful pregnancies.

Our Female Infertility Treatment Services Include:

  • Comprehensive Fertility Assessment: Hormonal Testing: Evaluation of hormone levels, including FSH, LH, AMH, and thyroid hormones.
  • Ovulation Induction: Medications like clomiphene citrate or letrozole to stimulate egg development and release.
  • Assisted Reproductive Techniques (ART): Intrauterine Insemination (IUI): Direct insertion of sperm into the uterus during ovulation.
  • Surgical Interventions: Hysteroscopic Surgery: Removal of uterine polyps, fibroids, or adhesions.
  • Lifestyle and Nutritional Counseling: Addressing lifestyle factors like diet, weight, stress, and exercise to improve fertility outcomes.

Conditions We Treat with Female Infertility Care:

  • Ovulation Disorders: Irregular or absent ovulation due to PCOS, hormonal imbalances, or premature ovarian insufficiency.
  • Tubal Factor Infertility: Blocked or damaged fallopian tubes caused by infections, endometriosis, or previous surgeries.
  • Endometriosis: Pain and fertility issues due to uterine tissue growing outside the uterus.
  • Uterine Abnormalities: Fibroids, polyps, adhesions, or structural issues impacting implantation.
  • Unexplained Infertility: Fertility challenges with no identifiable cause after standard evaluations.
