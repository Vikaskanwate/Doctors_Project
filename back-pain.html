Back Pain

What is Back Pain?

Back pain is one of the most common medical conditions, affecting people of all ages. It can occur in the upper, middle, or lower back due to various causes, including muscle strain, poor posture, herniated discs, spinal injuries, or underlying medical conditions like arthritis or osteoporosis. Back pain can range from a dull ache to sharp, debilitating pain, affecting mobility and quality of life. Proper diagnosis and timely treatment can help alleviate pain, restore mobility, and prevent chronic issues.

Our Back Pain Services Include:

  • Comprehensive Back Pain Diagnosis – Thorough evaluation using advanced imaging techniques like X-rays, MRIs, and CT scans to identify the root cause of back pain.
  • Non-Surgical Treatment Options – Physical therapy, medication, and lifestyle changes to relieve back pain without surgery.
  • Spinal Injections – Corticosteroid injections to reduce inflammation and relieve pain caused by herniated discs or nerve compression.
  • Minimally Invasive Back Surgery – Arthroscopic procedures for conditions like spinal stenosis, herniated discs, and sciatica, ensuring faster recovery and minimal scarring.
  • Posture Correction Therapy – Customized therapy sessions to improve posture, reducing the strain on the back and preventing recurring pain.

Benefits of Back Pain Treatment:

  • Pain Relief – Effective treatments provide immediate and long-term relief from acute and chronic back pain.
  • Restored Mobility – Back pain treatment helps patients regain normal movement, allowing them to return to their daily activities without discomfort.
  • Improved Posture and Spinal Health – Treatments focus on improving posture and strengthening the back muscles, reducing the risk of future issues.
  • Prevent Chronic Pain – Early intervention can prevent acute pain from developing into a chronic condition.
  • Minimally Invasive Options – Advanced surgical techniques offer quick recovery times and reduced post-operative pain.

Conditions We Treat with Back Pain Care:

  • Muscle Strains and Sprains – Overuse or sudden movements can cause muscle strains, leading to back pain and stiffness.
  • Herniated Discs – A slipped or bulging disc that puts pressure on the spinal nerves, causing back pain, numbness, and weakness.
  • Spinal Stenosis – Narrowing of the spinal canal that can cause nerve compression and pain in the back and legs.
  • Sciatica – Pain radiating from the lower back down the legs, often caused by nerve compression.
  • Arthritis – Degenerative joint disease that affects the spinal joints, causing back pain, stiffness, and reduced mobility.
