Neck Pain

What is Neck Pain?

Neck pain is a common condition that can result from muscle strain, poor posture, injuries, or underlying spinal issues. The neck (cervical spine) supports the weight of the head and provides mobility, making it prone to strain and injury. Neck pain can range from mild discomfort to severe, debilitating pain, often radiating to the shoulders, arms, and back. Early diagnosis and treatment can reduce pain, restore mobility, and prevent chronic neck conditions.

Our Neck Pain Services Include:

  • Comprehensive Neck Pain Diagnosis – Thorough evaluation using X-rays, MRIs, and CT scans to identify the root cause of neck pain.
  • Physical Therapy for Neck Pain – Customized therapy sessions to strengthen neck muscles, improve mobility, and reduce pain.
  • Cervical Spine Injections – Targeted injections to relieve nerve compression and reduce inflammation in the cervical spine.
  • Minimally Invasive Neck Surgery – For herniated discs, cervical stenosis, or nerve impingement causing severe neck pain.
  • Posture Correction Therapy – Postural assessments and correction exercises to prevent neck strain caused by poor posture.

Benefits of Neck Pain Treatment:

  • Pain Relief – Effective treatments provide immediate and long-term relief from acute and chronic back pain.
  • Improved Mobility and Flexibility – Neck pain treatment helps patients regain normal movement, improving quality of life.
  • Reduced Nerve Compression – Treatments relieve nerve compression in the cervical spine, preventing pain, numbness, and tingling sensations.
  • Posture Improvement – Treatment programs focus on correcting posture, reducing the risk of recurrent neck pain.
  • Minimally Invasive Options – Advanced surgical techniques provide quick recovery times with minimal scarring.

Conditions We Treat with Neck Pain Care:

  • Cervical Muscle Strains and Sprains – Overuse or sudden movements can cause muscle strain, leading to neck stiffness and pain.
  • Herniated Cervical Discs – A slipped disc in the neck that causes nerve compression, leading to pain, numbness, and weakness.
  • Cervical Spondylosis – Age-related wear and tear in the cervical spine, leading to stiffness, pain, and reduced mobility.
  • Cervical Stenosis – Narrowing of the cervical spinal canal that can cause nerve impingement and pain radiating to the arms.
  • Whiplash Injuries – Neck injuries caused by sudden jerking motions, commonly seen in car accidents.
