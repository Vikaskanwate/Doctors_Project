What is Menopause?

Menopause is a natural biological process that marks the end of a woman's reproductive years. It typically occurs around the age of 50 but can vary for each individual. Menopause is defined as the point when a woman has gone 12 consecutive months without a menstrual period. It signals a decrease in the production of hormones like estrogen and progesterone, leading to the cessation of menstruation and the end of fertility.

Our Menopause Advice and Support Services Include:

  • Comprehensive Assessment: Initial consultation to evaluate symptoms, medical history, and lifestyle factors to develop an individualized plan.
  • Hormone Replacement Therapy (HRT): Estrogen and Progesterone Therapy: Hormone replacement to manage hot flashes, night sweats, and other symptoms caused by hormonal imbalances.
    Bioidentical Hormone Therapy: A more natural approach using hormones that are chemically identical to those produced by the body.
  • Non-Hormonal Treatment Options: Medications for Symptom Relief: Antidepressants, anti-anxiety medications, and other prescriptions to manage mood swings, anxiety, and hot flashes.
    Herbal Supplements and Natural Remedies: Options like black cohosh, evening primrose oil, and other alternatives to support symptom relief.
  • Dietary and Lifestyle Modifications: Balanced Nutrition: Guidance on maintaining a healthy, well-balanced diet rich in calcium, vitamin D, and other essential nutrients for bone health and general well-being.
    Exercise Recommendations: Tailored physical activity plans to support weight management, reduce stress, and improve mood.
    Sleep Hygiene: Tips and strategies to improve sleep quality, which is often disrupted during menopause.

Benefits of Menopause Advice and Treatment:

  • Symptom Management: Helps reduce or eliminate common symptoms like hot flashes, mood swings, night sweats, and vaginal dryness.
  • Improved Emotional Well-Being: Alleviates feelings of anxiety, irritability, or depression through hormonal and lifestyle interventions.
  • Enhanced Quality of Life: Supports overall health, helping women continue to enjoy active and fulfilling lives during and after menopause.
  • Bone and Heart Health: Prevents complications like osteoporosis and cardiovascular disease, which are more common after menopause.
  • Informed Decision-Making: Provides women with the knowledge and tools to make empowered decisions about their health and well-being during this phase of life.

Conditions We Address in Menopause Care:

  • Hot Flashes and Night Sweats: Common symptoms caused by hormonal fluctuations, often requiring hormonal or non-hormonal treatments.
  • Osteoporosis: Decreased bone density leading to an increased risk of fractures due to lower estrogen levels.
  • Vaginal Dryness and Atrophy: Decreased estrogen can lead to vaginal dryness, discomfort during sex, and urinary tract symptoms.
  • Mood Swings and Depression: Emotional and psychological symptoms like irritability, anxiety, and depression due to hormonal changes.
  • Sleep Disturbances: Difficulty sleeping, including insomnia, caused by hormonal imbalances or other menopause-related symptoms.
  • Urinary Incontinence: A common issue for many women during menopause due to weakening pelvic floor muscles and hormonal changes.
